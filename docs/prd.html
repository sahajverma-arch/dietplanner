1. Overview

Dietitians run a 55–65 minute first-counselling call where they collect a client's goals, medical history, actual food day, preferences, training and behaviour patterns — then traditionally spend significant additional time manually composing a personalised 1-week diet chart, formatting it, and repeating a lighter version weekly per client. The LEANR Diet Platform removes the composition and formatting work while raising the clinical bar: the dietitian fills the structured LeanR Premium counselling live during the call (option-select, conversational — options are not read out), records their professional strategy hypothesis, and the platform's AI independently reviews that hypothesis against the complete client profile before generating a restriction-aware, macro-annotated 7-day plan whose numbers are grounded in the ICMR-NIN/INDB and USDA food databases — delivered as a branded, client-ready PDF in about a minute. Weekly follow-ups take a short check-in and produce the next adjusted week.

2. Goals & Non-Goals

Goals

  • Cut per-client plan preparation time from hours to minutes while keeping the dietitian in clinical control.
  • Run the full LeanR Premium Final New Client First Counselling (Q1–Q105, 12 sections) as a live, schema-driven form with conditional follow-ups, mandatory-question gating, live red flags and a 100-point counselling quality audit.
  • Guarantee plans respect hard constraints: diet pattern (veg / non-veg / vegan / eggetarian / Jain), allergies (zero tolerance), intolerances, dislikes, doctor instructions, day-of-week religious food rules, and medical conditions.
  • Treat the dietitian's strategy as a professional hypothesis: the AI independently analyses the complete client profile and supports, modifies, replaces, or pauses it — never blindly obeys, never competes.
  • Ground every plan's calories and macros in credible food databases (INDB — ICMR-NIN IFCT-based; USDA SR Legacy) instead of model estimates.
  • Produce a premium, client-shareable PDF with per-meal calories/macros, daily totals and a weekly summary.
  • Fit the ops workflow: each dietitian's scheduled counselling calls (from the ops Google Sheet) appear in-app with a one-click "Start Counselling" that pre-fills the client and closes the loop on completion.
  • Zero data leakage between dietitians; never lose intake data mid-call (continuous autosave).

Non-Goals (v2)

  • No client-facing portal or login — the dietitian is the only user; plans reach clients as PDFs.
  • No payment/subscription management or CRM features.
  • No medical diagnosis — the AI flags risks and adapts plans, but the dietitian reviews every plan and owns escalation.

3. Users

Primary — the practising dietitian (signs in with a @fitelo.co Google account; onboarding captures employee code and phone for ops mapping). Handles 20–100 active clients, runs multiple counselling calls per day from the Today's Activity schedule, needs to capture details fast mid-conversation, and sends every client a weekly chart.

Secondary — the admin. Read-only visibility across dietitians (clients, plans, activity) plus a manual "Sync sheet now" control.

4. User Journeys

4.1 First counselling (during the call)

  1. Dietitian signs in with Google and opens Today's Activity — their scheduled calls for the day, synced nightly from the ops sheet — and clicks Start Counselling on a row (client code and name pre-fill), or starts a blank + New Counselling.
  2. Runs the LeanR Premium counselling conversationally, mainly selecting options: goal & deeper motivation, body history, medical safety, digestion, the actual food day (interactive meal timeline), preferences & feasibility, training/protein/recovery, behaviour, lifestyle, dropout risk & coaching profile. Red flags and the quality score update live; everything autosaves.
  3. Records the professional assessment (Section 11 — limiting factors, targets, timeline, energy/protein/carb/fat strategy, diet structure, lifestyle-fit score) and the client strategy discussion (Section 12).
  4. Clicks Generate Week 1 Diet Plan. The AI first runs its independent clinical review — on a Pause decision (missing information / safety concern) nothing is saved, the reasons are shown, and the counselling stays a draft to fix and resubmit. Otherwise the plan is generated honouring the review, grounded against the food database, rendered to PDF and stored; the person becomes a saved client and the appointment is marked Completed.

4.2 Weekly follow-up

  1. Dietitian opens the client page, records weight, adherence, energy, hunger, complaints and notes.
  2. Clicks Generate Week N Plan — the AI follows the LeanR weekly loop (Previous Strategy → Execution → Feedback → Result → Interpretation → Updated Strategy → Next Weekly Diet), sees last week's menu for variety, and produces the adjusted week with a fresh PDF in the plan history.

6. Non-Functional Requirements

IDRequirement
NFR-1Isolation: Postgres Row Level Security on every table (dietitian_id = auth.uid(); appointments matched by employee code) and folder-scoped storage policies; one dietitian can never read another's data, even with the public API key. Admin access is read-only via policy.
NFR-2Clinical safety: an allergen surviving all corrective retries aborts the plan; escalated red flags are always visible before generation; a doctor's instruction overrides plan design; Pause decisions block generation until resolved.
NFR-3Secrets: the NVIDIA API key, service-role key (scripts/sync only) and sheet credentials are server-side only.
NFR-4Performance: review + generation typically ≤120 s; hard cap 300 s with explicit failure and retry path.
NFR-5Resilience: per-call AI timeouts, automatic fallback model, constrained JSON decoding, schema validation with corrective retries, split (two-call) generation, non-fatal grounding, and a review failure degrading to generation-without-review rather than blocking the dietitian.
NFR-6Data safety: no intake loss on network failure, generation failure, Pause, or tab close (autosaved drafts + preserved clients).
NFR-7Deployability: single-command Vercel deploy; database provisioned by sequential SQL migrations (0001–0006); food data seeded by one script.

7. Success Metrics

  • Median time from "counselling complete" to "PDF in hand" ≤ 2.5 minutes (review + generation + grounding + render).
  • ≥ 95% of generation attempts produce a valid plan (including automatic retries/fallback).
  • 100% of shipped plans free of client allergens (enforced — refusal instead of shipping).
  • ≥ 80% of meals grounded in the reference food database.
  • Zero cross-tenant data access incidents; zero reported intake-data losses during live calls.

8. Future Scope (v3 candidates)

  • Blood-report upload → AI extraction → dietitian verification (the flow the LeanR Premium spec prefers for Q20).
  • Retrieval into the prompt (RAG): feed candidate foods from the reference DB (filtered by cuisine, diet pattern, restrictions) into generation so every item matches the database.
  • WhatsApp/email delivery of the weekly PDF directly to the client.
  • Meal-level swap suggestions without regenerating the full week.
  • Client progress analytics (weight trend, adherence history) and searchable food selectors in the counselling form.
  • Multi-dietitian clinic roles beyond read-only admin.
Review policy: the dietitian's Section 11 strategy is a professional hypothesis; the AI independently tests it and every AI-generated plan is presented to the dietitian before it reaches a client. The PDF footer states the plan was generated with AI assistance and reviewed by the dietitian, and is not a substitute for medical advice.
